The challenge

A legally complex journey hiding behind a confusing interface

Outfox Energy needed a seamless onboarding experience for users exploring tariffs, comparing plans, and switching providers. The existing flow had significant usability gaps — dense forms, unclear pricing, and weak trust signals caused high abandonment.

"The challenge was to simplify a legally complex utility signup journey without removing critical compliance information."

Design brief, Outfox Energy project
My role on this project

Wireframes, user flows & final UI

I was responsible for two key phases of this project. First, I mapped and wireframed the entire user flow, defining the 5-step structure, the information architecture of each screen, and how trust signals would be sequenced across the journey.

Then I took those wireframes through to final UI, translating the structural decisions into a polished, production-ready visual design that aligned with Outfox's brand while meeting the usability requirements established in the flow phase.

Screen walkthrough

What each screen does well

A screen-by-screen look at the design decisions, trust signals, and UX thinking behind each step of the live journey.

01

Quote tool — the entry point

Users specify energy type, location and usage to generate personalised tariff quotes. This screen sets the tone for the entire journey.

outfox.energy
Progressive disclosure

Energy type selection is presented first as a simple toggle, before asking for location. Chunking decisions this way reduces initial cognitive load and keeps the tool feeling approachable rather than form-heavy.

Trust signal placement

The Trustpilot 4.7 rating sits just below the CTA — precisely where hesitant users look before committing. At nearly 48,000 reviews this is one of the strongest social proof assets in the UK energy sector.

Address lookup UX

The "Select your address (18 available)" dropdown confirms the postcode has been recognized and gives users control over the exact address, preventing supply-to-wrong-address errors downstream.

02

Tariff selection — the decision moment

Users choose between Fixed, Smart and Variable plans. The most cognitively demanding screen in the journey.

outfox.energy
"Best value" anchoring

The recommended tariff is visually elevated with a badge and heavier border. This anchoring technique reduces decision paralysis without forcing the user's hand, a deliberate wireframe decision carried through to final UI.

Consistent card structure

Every tariff card follows the same layout, price, feature ticks, Select CTA. Consistency lets users scan and compare plans without re-learning the layout on each card.

Variable tariff transparency

"Fox Standard Dual" is listed separately and honestly describes following the Ofgem Price Cap. This transparency builds long-term brand trust even where it doesn't maximize short-term conversion.

03

Your details — building the account

Collects personal info, supply date, homeowner status, EV/solar ownership and Priority Services eligibility.

outfox.energy
Tariff persistence

The selected tariff summary card stays anchored at the top throughout the form. This was a deliberate wireframe decision, users filling in personal details need a constant reminder of what they're signing up for.

Flexible supply start date

Offering a suggested date alongside a "let me pick" option gives users control over the transition, particularly important for users mid-contract who need to time the switch carefully.

14-day cooling off — placed deliberately

The cancellation policy is surfaced right alongside the supply start date, exactly when commitment anxiety peaks. Placing it here reduces hesitation at the critical moment rather than burying it in T&Cs.

04

Payment — Direct Debit setup

The highest-trust moment in the journey. Bank details collected via Direct Debit with full schedule transparency shown upfront.

outfox.energy
Payment schedule upfront

First payment date, amount, subsequent date and amount are all shown before the user enters a single bank detail. This was the key UI decision on this screen, transparency before the ask.

Direct Debit guarantee

The DD logo and Guarantee link are prominently displayed, a well-understood consumer protection signal in the UK that meaningfully reduces anxiety around automated payments.

Explicit account holder confirmation

Presenting the "I am the account holder" step as a clear declaration, not buried in T&Cs — makes users feel in control and properly informed at the most legally significant step.

05

Review & confirm — the final commitment

Complete summary of tariff, payment schedule, supply references and unit rates before the Switch Now CTA.

outfox.energy
Complete information summary

Everything surfaces here, tariff name, Direct Debit schedule, meter type, MPAN/MPRN references, unit rates and standing charges. Pre-commit transparency is both good UX and required by Ofgem standards.

Annual cost visibility

Both estimated monthly (£150) and yearly (£1,550) figures are shown, giving users the mental model of their full annual commitment before they press Switch Now.

14-day reminder at point of commitment

The cooling-off period is restated just above the Switch Now button, the highest-anxiety moment. This last-mile reassurance was a conscious design decision to remove the final psychological barrier.

06

Success — a warm welcome

The journey is complete. This screen reinforces the user's decision and sets clear expectations for what happens next.

outfox.energy
Brand mascot moment

The Outfox fox mascot appears for the first time on the success screen, a warm, playful touch that makes the confirmation feel like a genuine milestone rather than just a system receipt.

Clear "what's next" framing

Rather than ending with a generic thank you, the screen immediately addresses the user's next concern: welcome pack via email. Proactive framing reduces post-signup anxiety.

Community language

"You're now part of a community who enjoys some of the most competitive tariffs" frames signup as joining something, not just buying something. A deliberate tone choice to build loyalty from day one.
